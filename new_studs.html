Inquire About Studs

Fully Threaded Studs

Fully threaded studs are threaded along their entire length, allowing for full engagement of nuts or tapped holes. They are versatile and used in a wide range of applications for bolting, clamping, and assembly.

Double End Studs

Double end studs have threads on both ends, with an unthreaded shank in the middle. One end is typically installed into a tapped hole, while the other end accepts a nut. Commonly used in machinery and automotive applications.

Tap End Studs (Example)

Tap end studs have a shorter thread length on one end (the tap end) designed to be screwed into a tapped hole, and a longer thread length on the other end (the nut end) for a nut. The unthreaded shank separates these sections.

Weld Studs (Example)

Weld studs are designed to be welded to a base material, providing a permanent threaded stud. They come in various forms, such as capacitor discharge (CD) weld studs and arc weld studs, for different applications and materials.
